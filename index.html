What Does the Product Do:


Nano C effectively nullifies the problem of having to hold your phone and eliminates any usage of cords through wireless charging. That means if someone wanted to listen to music on their iPhone 7 or better (or another phone that uses the charging port and a dongle for headphones) they could charge their phone and still be able to use their headphones. The nano suction technology enables users to stick he/she phone anywhere. For example, when on the road, users can easily put he/she phone on their windshield without having the obstruction of another phone attachment. Another example is when the user is watching a video while cooking, he/she does not have to place his/her phone in an area where the phone may potentially become damaged or worsened. Additionally, there will be no adhesive substances left behind as there is with tape and sticky notes. Instead of utilizing adhesives, we, as a group, decided to apply mechanical suction. This mechanical suction technology If an individual were to take a group picture, but there was no one else around, they could easily stick their phone to a nearby flat wall or structure. Our case solves many everyday problems essentially combining two unique ideas to become the best.


What Other People Think:


To test the marketability of our product, we conducted a survey with over 60 respondents. From our data, we gathered that most people, around 60% of the respondents, were tired of carrying their phones for long periods of time or when they were busy doing something else as well. This data means that the nano suction feature on our phone case would be marketable because it would allow the user to place their phone on a vertical surface such as a wall without it falling. This means the user can free the hand holding the phone in order to work on other tasks. One question that many of the respondents have asked is if our phone cases will accidentally stick to pockets. However, nano suction only attaches to non-porous surfaces such as walls and as a result, the porous pocket will not become stuck to the phone case. Another problem that the users feared would happen, was the phone case would be very hard to remove from the wall. However, this will not occur with our case because of a simple property in physics. The vacuum created by the phone case is strong enough to lift itself and the phone in the process. An easy way to remove the phone from the wall is to simply peel the phone off from one corner, pulling towards you and perpendicular to the wall. Air will rush it because of diffusion and the vacuum will be lost. Therefore, there is no need to worry about the phone case getting stuck in a pocket because a pocket is too porous for the phone to attach.
